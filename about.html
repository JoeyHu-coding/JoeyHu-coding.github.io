About

Biography, background, and contact.

Urban environmental researcher working at the intersection of climate adaptation, green infrastructure, remote sensing, and spatial equity.

Bio

Jinyu Hu is an M.S. candidate in Resources and Environment in the Department of Environmental Science and Engineering at Fudan University, advised by Prof. Zhaowu Yu. His research examines how urban spatial form, green infrastructure, and climate stress interact to shape environmental exposure, adaptation capacity, and social equity. Methodologically, his work draws on remote sensing, spatial econometrics, urban ecological modeling, GeoAI, and multi-source urban data.

Jinyu's recent work includes global urban shade mapping, green-space exposure inequality, habitat-quality modeling under rapid urbanization, and nonlinear cooling effects of urban green spaces. His first-author publications include articles in Ecological Indicators and Science of The Total Environment, and his publication record has received more than 440 Google Scholar citations.

Background

Jinyu received his B.S. in Landscape Architecture from Nanjing Forestry University, graduating summa cum laude with the highest rank in his cohort. This training grounds his current environmental research in landscape planning, urban ecology, spatial representation, and design-oriented thinking. At Fudan University, his graduate work extends this background toward urban climate modeling, three-dimensional urban environmental analysis, environmental economics, and climate-health evidence.

He has also worked with the Institute of Energy, Environment and Economics at Tsinghua University, the Lancet Countdown Asia Center, Osaka University's Yabuki Lab, and several urban ecology and climate-related research projects. These experiences connect city-scale spatial analysis with policy-relevant questions in clean energy, CCUS suitability, climate risks, health, and nature-based solutions.
