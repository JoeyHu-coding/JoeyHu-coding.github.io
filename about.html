Bio

Jinyu Hu is an M.S. candidate in Resources and Environment in the Department of Environmental Science and Engineering at Fudan University. His research examines how urban spatial form, green infrastructure, and climate stress interact to shape environmental exposure, adaptation capacity, and social equity.

His work draws on remote sensing, spatial econometrics, urban ecological modeling, GeoAI, street-view imagery, and multi-source urban data. Current and recent topics include global urban shade mapping, green-space exposure inequality, habitat-quality modeling under rapid urbanization, nonlinear cooling effects of urban green spaces, and climate-change and health evidence synthesis.

Jinyu's undergraduate training in landscape architecture at Nanjing Forestry University grounds this work in landscape ecology, site-scale spatial thinking, and design-oriented environmental analysis. His undergraduate research produced two first-author SCI papers, including an ESI Highly Cited Paper, and his publication record has received over 450 citations.
