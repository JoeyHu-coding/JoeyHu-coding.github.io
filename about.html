Bio

Jinyu Hu is an M.S. candidate in Resources and Environment in the Department of Environmental Science and Engineering at Fudan University. His current research examines how Earth-system change interacts with urban systems, human settlements, infrastructure, climate risk, and environmental governance.

His research trajectory has moved from green-space exposure and landscape ecology toward broader urban-environment and climate-risk questions. This transition connects his earlier training in landscape architecture and ecological modeling with current work on urban light and thermal environments, infrastructure demand, energy systems, data-center development, and environmental policy.

Methodologically, Jinyu works with urban and environmental remote sensing, spatial statistics, GeoAI, deep learning, large-language-model-assisted text analysis, and multi-source urban data. His earlier first-author work on green-gray imbalance, habitat quality, and landscape patterns remains an important foundation for this current shift. His publications include two first-author SCI papers, an ESI Highly Cited Paper, and over 450 citations.
