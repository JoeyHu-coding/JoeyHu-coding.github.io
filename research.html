Research Agenda

Urban human settlement environments from local scale to planetary scale.

My research asks what future human settlement environments should look like, and how their infrastructure, exposure, perception, and climate risks can be measured and improved across scales.

Overall Positioning

Human settlement environment modeling, sensing, and infrastructure analysis

Human settlement environments are the everyday living environments formed by built space, infrastructure, ecological space, climate stressors, and human activity. I study them as concrete, measurable conditions rather than as an abstract coupling problem.

My work moves from local scale to planetary scale, from street and building geometry, light and thermal conditions, green exposure, and infrastructure systems to planetary scale datasets and comparisons across cities. Earth system processes and urban system transformations remain important context, but they are used to explain settlement conditions, risks, and livability.

Central Question

What will future human settlement environments look like, and how can their infrastructure, exposure, perception, and climate risks be measured, modeled, and improved across scales?

Core object
Human settlement environments
Priority lenses
Infrastructure modeling, environmental sensing, and climate risk
Scale
Local observation to planetary comparison
Theme 01

Core contribution

Human Settlement Environment Modeling: Urban Light, Shade, and Day and Night Cycles

Urban light environments offer a detailed way to model human settlements. Daylight, shadow, and nighttime light are physical signals, but their settlement meaning is produced by buildings, street canyons, infrastructure, human activity, and exposure patterns.

3D urban form and shade modeling

High resolution characterization of sunlight and shadow conditions within 3D urban space over a full annual cycle.

Day and night environmental sensing

Integration of daytime sun and shade conditions with nighttime light data to analyze complete light environment dynamics in human settlements.

Perception, exposure, and settlement quality

Evaluation of urban form, environmental exposure, spatial inequality, health-relevant conditions, and everyday livability.

Theme 02

Infrastructure analysis

Emerging Settlement Infrastructure: Data Centers and Environmental Footprints

I study data centers as emerging human settlement infrastructure. They connect land development, electricity demand, water use, cooling, carbon emissions, local environmental burdens, and the spatial organization of future urban life.

The focus is not energy policy alone, but how digital infrastructure changes settlement resource relationships and planning constraints. My analysis links infrastructure location, clean power availability, PUE, climate background, environmental footprint, and population relevant exposure.

Theme 03

Risk and resilience

Climate Risk, Infrastructure Resilience, and Human Settlement Quality

My climate risk work examines how human settlements experience heat exposure, flooding, wildfire, drought, tropical cyclones, and overlapping hazards through infrastructure, land development, and everyday exposure conditions. This is where Earth system change most directly enters my work on human settlement environments.

The central concern is whether settlement environments and infrastructure systems can maintain safety, functionality, and livability under climate stress.

Theme 04

Ecological exposure and welfare

Green Coverage, Land Use, and Habitat Quality

This theme builds from my earlier research in green space exposure and landscape ecology. I study how land development and spatial planning transform ecological space patterns, and how those changes affect settlement quality, human green exposure, habitat quality, ecological welfare, and environmental equity.

The work connects land-use change, urban expansion, landscape configuration, green-gray imbalance, accessibility, and planning-oriented evaluation.

Methodological Integration

Remote sensing, social sensing, GeoAI, deep learning, and large language models

Methods are not a separate research object here. I use them as an integrated toolkit for observing, modeling, predicting, and interpreting human settlement environments and their infrastructure, exposure, perception, and risk conditions.

Environmental Remote Sensing

Observes land-surface conditions, urban form, infrastructure, green space, temperature, nighttime lights, and exposure across scales.

Social Sensing

Uses social-media analysis, volunteered geographic information, GIS-linked human mobility data, and other human activity records to interpret settlement environments and urban dynamics.

GeoAI and Deep Learning

Infers geographic features, fills spatial-data gaps, detects complex patterns, predicts environmental conditions, and supports risk modeling.

Large Language Models

Supports policy text analysis, environmental policy synthesis, literature mining, and evidence aggregation.
