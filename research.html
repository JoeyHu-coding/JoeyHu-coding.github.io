Research

Earth-system change, urban systems, infrastructure, and environmental risk.

01

Earth Systems & Urban Systems

I study how large-scale environmental change is expressed through city systems, including urban form, land-surface processes, infrastructure demand, energy consumption, environmental exposure, and adaptation capacity.

02

Human Settlements: Light & Heat

I examine urban human environments, especially light and thermal conditions. A current manuscript develops a global urban shade dataset across 1,487 major cities and studies the mismatch between shadows and shortwave solar radiation.

03

Climate Risk, Infrastructure & Energy

Recent work extends toward climate-risk assessment, clean-energy infrastructure, data-center development, power and energy constraints, CCUS suitability, and climate-change and health evidence synthesis.

04

Remote Sensing, GeoAI & Policy

My work uses urban and environmental remote sensing, GeoAI, deep learning, spatial statistics, and LLM-assisted text analysis to connect environmental data with policy-relevant climate and urban questions.

Current Questions

  • How does Earth-system change reshape urban systems, infrastructure demand, and environmental risk?
  • How well are urban shadows aligned with radiative demand across global cities?
  • How should cities evaluate climate-risk, energy, power-system, and data-center development constraints together?
  • How can remote sensing, GeoAI, deep learning, and LLM-assisted evidence mining support policy-relevant environmental research?

Methods & Approaches

  • Remote sensing and geospatial computing: Google Earth Engine, ENVI, ArcGIS, digital surface models, geostationary satellite data, and street-view imagery.
  • Spatial and computational modeling: Python, R, GeoAI, deep learning, spatial econometrics, GTWR, MGWR, and multi-source urban data integration.
  • Policy and evidence analysis: environmental policy text mining, LLM-assisted literature mining, climate-health evidence synthesis, and scenario analysis.
  • Urban ecology foundation: green exposure, accessibility, InVEST, Linkage Mapper, Fragstats, Conefor, habitat quality, and ecological corridor analysis.

Green Space & Landscape Ecology Foundation

Green-space exposure and landscape ecology were the core of my earlier research and remain the empirical foundation for my current urban-environment work. This line includes green-gray imbalance, daily accessible greenery, habitat quality, landscape-pattern mechanisms, ecological connectivity, and ecosystem-service assessment.

Transition

The current direction builds from this foundation toward broader questions about human settlements under climate change: how urban form, infrastructure, energy demand, environmental policy, and computational sensing methods jointly shape risk and adaptation.
