Research

Urban environmental modeling for climate adaptation, exposure, and equity.

01

Urban Climate & Shade

I study how three-dimensional urban form shapes sunlight, shadow, radiative demand, and heat exposure. A current manuscript develops a global urban shade dataset across 1,487 major cities and examines the mismatch between shadows and shortwave solar radiation.

02

Green Exposure & Equity

I examine how residents access and experience green spaces under rapid urbanization, and how green-gray imbalance, daily accessibility, street-view greenery, and socio-spatial inequality affect exposure to urban environmental benefits.

03

Urban Ecology & Habitat

I use landscape ecology and spatial models to analyze land-use change, landscape patterns, habitat quality, ecological corridors, and ecosystem-service capacity in rapidly urbanizing regions.

04

Climate, Energy & Health

Recent work extends these interests toward clean-energy infrastructure, CCUS suitability, climate-risk evidence mining, and climate-change and health policy synthesis for reports and briefs.

Current Questions

  • How well are urban shadows aligned with radiative demand across global cities?
  • How does rapid urbanization alter the probability of daily green-space exposure?
  • Which combinations of vegetation, buildings, and land-use patterns produce more equitable cooling and ecological benefits?
  • How can remote sensing, spatial statistics, and environmental text mining support policy-relevant climate adaptation research?

Methods & Approaches

  • Remote sensing and geospatial computing: Google Earth Engine, ENVI, ArcGIS, digital surface models, geostationary satellite data, and street-view imagery.
  • Spatial analysis and modeling: GTWR, MGWR, spatial econometrics, spatial syntax, buffer analysis, least-two-step move search, and exposure metrics.
  • Urban ecology: InVEST, Linkage Mapper, Fragstats, Conefor, habitat-quality assessment, ecological corridors, and ecosystem-service modules.
  • Computational analysis: Python, R, GeoAI, deep learning, large-language-model-assisted text analysis, and evidence synthesis.
