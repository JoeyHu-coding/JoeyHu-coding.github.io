Research Agenda

Human-settlement environments at the interface of Earth systems and urban systems.

My research asks how planetary processes, urban form, infrastructure expansion, land development, and environmental governance jointly shape the environments in which people live.

Overall Positioning

Urban-Earth system coupling through human-settlement environments

Cities occupy a limited share of land, but they concentrate population, infrastructure, energy demand, and environmental exposure. They cannot be understood apart from Earth systems: solar radiation, surface-energy balance, climate disturbance, land-cover change, and ecological processes all enter urban life through built form, infrastructure, land use, and policy.

I use human-settlement environments as the central interface: broader than a narrow city boundary, but still focused on the everyday environmental conditions, risks, and welfare consequences experienced by people.

Central Question

How do Earth-system processes and urban-system transformations interact across scales, and how can those interactions be measured, evaluated, and governed to improve human-settlement quality?

Interface
Human-settlement environments
Objects
Light, infrastructure, land, habitats, and climate risk
Approach
Remote sensing, GeoAI, spatial modeling, and policy analysis
Theme 01

Current core contribution

Urban Light Environments: Daylight, Shadow, and Day-Night Light Cycles

Urban light environments form a direct link between Earth-system rhythms and three-dimensional urban form. Daylight and shadow are produced by planetary motion and solar geometry, but their settlement consequences are mediated by buildings, street canyons, morphology, and land-surface context.

Global sunlight and shadow dataset

One of the first global-scale datasets characterizing sunlight and shadow conditions within three-dimensional urban space over a full annual cycle.

Day-night light cycles

Integration of daytime sun-shadow conditions and nighttime-light data to analyze complete day-night light-environment dynamics.

Settlement quality and exposure

Evaluation of urban form, environmental exposure, spatial inequality, health-relevant conditions, and everyday settlement quality.

Theme 02

Governance and emerging infrastructure

Environmental Policy and Emerging Infrastructure: Data Centers

I use data centers as an entry point for connecting environmental measurement with policy implementation. As fast-growing infrastructure, they reshape electricity demand, clean-energy requirements, land development, water use, carbon emissions, and local environmental burdens.

My research links their environmental footprint and population-relevant impacts with renewable-energy supply, power-system constraints, climate background, and policy measures.

Theme 03

Ecological space and settlement welfare

Green Exposure, Land Use, and Habitat Quality

This theme builds from my earlier research in green-space exposure and landscape ecology. I study how land development and spatial planning transform ecological-space patterns, and how those changes affect human green exposure, habitat quality, ecological welfare, and environmental equity.

The work connects land-use change, urban expansion, landscape configuration, green-gray imbalance, accessibility, and planning-oriented evaluation.

Theme 04

Extreme disturbance and adaptation

Climate Risk, Infrastructure Resilience, and Human-Settlement Quality

My climate-risk work expands from earlier heat-environment research toward compound risks affecting human settlements, including heat exposure, flooding, wildfire, drought, tropical cyclones, and overlapping hazards.

The central concern is whether infrastructure systems and settlement environments can maintain safety, functionality, and livability under climate stress.

Methodological Integration

Remote sensing, GeoAI, computer vision, deep learning, and large language models

Methods are not a separate research object here. I use them as an integrated toolkit for observing, modeling, predicting, and interpreting urban-Earth system interactions and their policy implications.

Environmental Remote Sensing

Observes land-surface conditions, urban form, green space, temperature, nighttime lights, and exposure across scales.

Computer Vision

Extracts three-dimensional morphology, spatial structure, built-environment features, and visual environmental information.

GeoAI and Deep Learning

Infers geographic features, fills spatial-data gaps, detects complex patterns, predicts environmental conditions, and supports risk modeling.

Large Language Models

Supports policy-text analysis, environmental-policy synthesis, literature mining, and evidence aggregation.
