2025 - Present Research Intern Tsinghua University

Clean-energy constraints, data-center siting, CCUS suitability, and climate-risk text analysis

At the Institute of Energy, Environment and Economics, Tsinghua University, I support research on future high-performance data-center development in China under clean-energy deployment and energy-consumption constraints. The work also includes suitability mapping for carbon capture, utilization and storage (CCUS), policy-text analysis, and large-scale environmental evidence mining.

Type
Energy-environment research, spatial suitability assessment, policy analysis
Contribution
Multi-source environmental data analysis, remote sensing, scenario assessment, LLM-assisted literature mining
Supervisor
Prof. Shiyan Chang
2024 - Present Project Member Lancet Countdown Asia Center

Climate change and health evidence monitoring for the Lancet Countdown China report

I contribute to the 2025 China report of the Lancet Countdown on health and climate change, and to thematic briefs on international climate-health evidence. My work includes collecting policy, academic, and event information, organizing evidence for major ministerial audiences, and producing structured reading reports.

2022 - 2024 Project Lead National Student Innovation Program

Evaluating and optimizing urban green-space accessibility from a social equity perspective under the "Park City" initiative

This funded project assessed unequal access to urban green-space resources and proposed optimization strategies to improve residents' green exposure. I led proposal writing, research design, spatial analysis, and manuscript preparation. The project received an excellent completion rating.

Methods
Buffer analysis, spatial syntax, least-two-step move search, street-view imagery, POI data
Funding
China National University Student Innovation Program, No. 202210298047Z; funding amount RMB 11,000

Methods
Literature review, spatial data collection, CiteSpace, bibliometric network analysis
Funding

2022 - 2024 Core Member National Student Innovation Program

Conservation and restoration of bird habitats in urban parks

Using Nanjing Xuanwu Lake Park as a case, this project analyzed urban habitat quality and ecological connectivity. My work focused on spatial data collection, habitat-quality assessment with InVEST, and ecological corridor delineation with Linkage Mapper.

Methods
Spatial data collection, InVEST habitat-quality modeling, Linkage Mapper ecological corridor analysis
Funding
China National University Student Innovation Program, No. 202210298048Z; funding amount RMB 8,000

Partners

Contribution
InVEST-based module development, green-space accessibility assessment, attractiveness evaluation, and comprehensive park-quality metrics
