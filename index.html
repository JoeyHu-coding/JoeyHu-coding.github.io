About

I study how urban form, green infrastructure, and climate stress shape environmental exposure, risk, and adaptation in rapidly changing cities.

My work combines remote sensing, spatial statistics, urban ecology, and environmental modeling to examine urban shade, green-space exposure, habitat quality, cooling effects, and climate-health evidence. I am currently a graduate researcher in the Department of Environmental Science and Engineering at Fudan University.

Before Fudan, I received a B.S. in Landscape Architecture from Nanjing Forestry University, where I worked on green-space accessibility, habitat-quality modeling, and landscape-pattern analysis. My undergraduate work produced two first-author SCI papers, including an ESI Highly Cited Paper, and my publications have received more than 440 Google Scholar citations.

Research Interests

  • Green-space accessibility, exposure, and environmental equity
  • Remote sensing, GeoAI, and spatial econometrics
