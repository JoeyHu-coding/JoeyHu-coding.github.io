About

I study how urban form, green infrastructure, and climate stress shape environmental exposure, risk, and adaptation in rapidly changing cities.

My work combines remote sensing, spatial statistics, urban ecology, and environmental modeling to examine urban shade, green-space exposure, habitat quality, cooling effects, and climate-health evidence. I am currently a graduate researcher in the Department of Environmental Science and Engineering at Fudan University, advised by Prof. Zhaowu Yu.

Before Fudan, I received a B.S. in Landscape Architecture from Nanjing Forestry University, where I worked on urban green-space accessibility, habitat quality, and landscape-pattern analysis. My publications have received more than 440 Google Scholar citations.
