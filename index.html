Profile

I study human settlement infrastructure and climate risk, with a focus on modeling and sensing urban living environments from local scale to planetary scale.

My research examines how built form, infrastructure systems, ecological space, and climate stress shape the conditions people experience in cities and settlements. Recent work focuses on urban light and thermal environments, environmental exposure, settlement quality, and infrastructure resilience using remote sensing, social sensing, spatial modeling, GeoAI, and evidence synthesis.

The work is grounded in urban and environmental science while remaining connected to Earth system processes and urban transformations, especially where climate change alters risk, infrastructure performance, and everyday livability. I move between detailed studies of urban morphology and global comparisons of settlement environments.

Methodologically, I combine urban and environmental remote sensing, social sensing, spatial analysis, GeoAI, deep learning, and LLM supported evidence synthesis. Earlier work on green space exposure and landscape ecology continues to inform my interest in environmental equity and planning. Multiple first author SCI papers have been published or are under review, and my publications have received over 450 citations.
