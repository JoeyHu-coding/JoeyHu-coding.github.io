Profile

I study cross-scale coupling between Earth systems and urban systems, with a focus on human-settlement infrastructure and climate risk.

My current work is organized around two connected directions. I examine Earth-system and urban-system coupling through urban light environments, thermal environments, climate risk, and infrastructure. In parallel, I mainly study environmental, energy, and economic policy questions, including data-center development under clean-energy constraints and environmental footprint assessment.

Methodologically, I work across urban and environmental remote sensing, spatial modeling, GeoAI, deep learning, and large-language-model-assisted evidence synthesis. I also conducted earlier research on green-space exposure and landscape ecology. Multiple first-author SCI papers have been published or are under review, and my publications have received over 450 citations.
